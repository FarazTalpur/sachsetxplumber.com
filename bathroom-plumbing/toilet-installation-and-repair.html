Frequently Asked Questions

Signs that your toilet may need repair include frequent clogs, constant running water, cracks in the porcelain, leaking from the base, or low flushing efficiency. If repairs are costly or frequent, it may be more economical to replace the toilet.

A constantly running toilet is often caused by a faulty flapper valve, which can be replaced easily. Check the fill valve and float for malfunctions as well. Adjusting or replacing these components might stop the toilet from running.

Installing a toilet can be done as a DIY project if you have plumbing experience and the right tools. However, hiring a professional is recommended to ensure it is installed correctly without leaks, especially if you're unfamiliar with plumbing work.

A wax ring is the traditional choice for sealing a toilet to the flange, but modern alternatives like rubber or foam rings are available and can provide a more durable seal with easier installation.

A leak from the base of the toilet is often due to a worn-out wax ring or improper installation. Replacing the seal between the toilet and the drain pipe should resolve this issue. Check that the bolts securing the toilet are tight as well.
