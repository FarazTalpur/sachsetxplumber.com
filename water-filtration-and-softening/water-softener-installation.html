Frequently Asked Questions

The ideal location for a water softener is near the main water line entry point to ensure all water entering the home is treated. It should also be near an electrical outlet and a drain for discharge during the regeneration cycle.

The size of a water softener is based on your household's water consumption and the hardness of your water. Generally, the capacity is measured in grains per gallon (GPG), and you should estimate your daily water usage by the number of people in your household multiplied by the average gallons used per day (typically 75 gallons per person).

A water softener requires three main connections: an inlet and outlet for water flow, and a drain line for wastewater created during regeneration. Ensure proper fittings and check valves are used to prevent any backflow or leaks.

A pre-filter is recommended if your water contains high levels of sediment, iron, or other contaminants that could clog or damage the resin bed of the water softener. Testing your water's quality will help in deciding if a pre-filter is necessary.

While a skilled DIY enthusiast can install a water softener themselves, it is often recommended to hire a professional to ensure correct installation, proper connections, and optimal performance. A professional can also help configure the system settings for your specific water conditions.
